Courtroom

Courtroom is a lightweight, verbose validation system written in TypeScript. It has an easy to understand syntax, using the metaphor of a real-life courtroom to help simplify the procedure.

There are a few examples below showing some of the different types of 'laws' (rules) you can apply to your 'defendants' (properties) with the 'courtroom' (object validator).

You can apply laws one-at-a-time, such as saying that the field 'password' must have a minimum length of 8 characters: 

  c.trial("password").laws.minLength(8);

Or you can chain them up, like saying that that the field 'favourite_day' must contain the word 'day' and must not be 'Tuesday'. 

  c.trial("favourite_day").laws.contains("day").not("Tuesday");

Interactive demos - with code!

Min 5 characters: 
  c.trial("min_5").laws.minLength(5);

Max 5 characters:
  c.trial("max_5").laws.maxLength(5);

Must contain 'hello': 
  c.trial("contains_hello").laws.contains("hello");

Must equal 'james':
  c.trial("equals_james").laws.is("james");

Must not equal 'paulo':
  c.trial("not_equals_paulo").laws.not("paulo");

Between 5 and 10 characters:
  c.trial("between_5_10").laws.minLength(5).maxLength(10);

Contains 'james', ' ' and 'monger':
  c.trial("contains_james_space_monger").laws.contains("james")
      .contains(" ").contains("monger");
